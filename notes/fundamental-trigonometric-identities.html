Conditional Equation vs Identity

Conditional Equation Identity
Difference True for some values of the variables True for all values of the variables, where both sides are defined
Example \[ \sin \theta = \cos \theta \] \[ \theta = 30, \hspace{0.25em} \theta \neq 45 \] \[ \tan \theta \cos \theta = \sin \theta \] where all values for \( \theta \) are defined, both sides.

Examples

Identities

Reciprocals

\[ \sec \theta = \frac{1}{\cos \theta} \] \[ \csc \theta = \frac{1}{\sin \theta} \] \[ \cot \theta = \frac{1}{\tan \theta} \]

Quotient

\[ \tan \theta = \frac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}, \hspace{2em} \cot \theta = \frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta} \]

Pythagorean

\[ \cos^2 \theta + \sin^2 \theta = 1 \] \[ 1 + \tan^2 \theta = \sec^2 \theta \] \[ \cot^2 \theta + 1 = \csc^2 \theta \]

Note how the 2nd and 3rd Pythagorean identities are variations of the first:

  • By dividing \( \cos^2 \theta \) for the second
  • By dividing \( \sin^2 \theta \) for the third

Even-Odd

\[ \cos(-\theta) = \cos \theta \] \[ \sin(-\theta) = -\sin \theta, \hspace{2em} \tan(-\theta) = -\tan \theta \]
