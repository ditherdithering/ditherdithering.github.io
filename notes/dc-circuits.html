Electric Circuit

A group of electrical components connected together to form a complete path for current.

The circuit where current flows in one direction is called DC circuit (DCC).

In DCC, it is the battery or the DC generator that supplies the voltage.

Series & Parallel Circuits

A circuit may be connected in series, parallel, or both.

Circuit diagrams may be used to analyze VIR.

In any combination of resistors, there is always a single equivalent resistor that can replace the combination without altering \( I_{\text{total}} \) and potential difference or \( V \).

  • Where 10Ω = 5Ω + 5Ω.

Series Circuits

Contains more than one electrical component connected one after the other in a single path.

\( V^+ \to R_1 \to R_2 \to R_3 \to V^- \)

Properties

  • \( R_{\text{total}} = \Sigma R \) on the current circuit
  • The current is constant/same in all parts.
  • The voltage is distributed in all parts of a circuit.

Parallel Circuits

Contains two or more electrical components connected across each other.

If one fails, the current still flows through as the components are independent.

Commonly found in household appliances.

Properties

  • \( R_t = \frac{1}{R_t} = \frac{1}{R_n} \)
  • The current is distributed in all parts of a circuit.
  • The voltage is constant/same in all parts.
