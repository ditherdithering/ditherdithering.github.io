Forming an angle is done via rotating a ray from its vertex from one position (initial side) of the angle to another (terminal side).

Angle in Standard Position

If it's drawn in the xy-plane where \( C \) is at the origin and its initial side is on the +x axis, it is a standard position angle.

The angles, either \( \theta, \hspace{0.3em} \alpha, \hspace{0.3em} \text{or} \hspace{0.3em} \beta \) in the following figures where \( n > 1 \) are for indication.

If the angle is formed by a counterclockwise rotation, the measure is positive. Otherwise, it's negative (formed by clockwise rotation).

  • Angular Measure
    • Amount of opening of an angle in its measure

Units of Measurements

  • Degree - \( n^\circ \)
    • One full rotation -> \( 360^\circ \)
  • Radian - rad
    • Measure of a central angle subtended by the arc of a circle whose length equals the circle radius

Clockwise -> -, Counter-CW -> +

Conversion Formula

\[ \text{Radians} \to \text{Degrees}: \hspace{1em} n\left(\frac{180}{\pi}\right)^\circ \]

\[ \text{Degrees} \to \text{Radians}: \hspace{1em} n\left(\frac{\pi}{180^\circ}\right)\text{ rad} \]

Where \( n \) is the value to be converted.

Coterminal Angles

\[ f(n) = [\theta + (n)(360)]^\circ \]

\[ f(n) = [\text{rad} + (n)(2\pi)] \hspace{0.3em} (\text{for radians}) \]

Angles in standard position with terminal sides coincide (mirror).

Example

Reference Angle

In any non-quadrantal \( \theta \) in a standard position, a reference angle \( \alpha \) exists as an acute angle formed by the terminal side of \( \theta \) and the x-axis; also known as the reference angle \( \alpha \).

Should the given value be in radians, convert to degrees and then to radians, or refer to Quadrant Reference Angles (Radian).

Quadrant Reference Angles (Degree)

\( I: \theta = \alpha \)
\( II: \alpha = 180 - \theta, \hspace{1.25cm} \theta \in \theta \mid 180 > \theta > 90 \)
\( III: \alpha = \theta - 180, \hspace{1.25cm} \theta \in \theta \mid 270 > \theta > 180 \)
\( IV: \alpha = 360 - \theta, \hspace{1.25cm} \theta \in \theta \mid 360 > \theta > 270 \)

Where \( \theta \mid a > \theta > b \) is the set of all \( \theta \) such that \( a > \theta > b \).

Quadrant Reference Angles (Radian)

\( I: \text{rad} = \alpha \)
\( II: \alpha = \pi - \text{rad}, \hspace{1.25cm} \text{rad} \in \text{rad} \mid \pi > \text{rad} > \frac{\pi}{2} \)
\( III: \alpha = \text{rad} - \pi, \hspace{1.25cm} \text{rad} \in \text{rad} \mid \frac{3\pi}{2} > \text{rad} > \pi \)
\( IV: \alpha = 2\pi - \text{rad}, \hspace{1.25cm} \text{rad} \in \text{rad} \mid 2\pi > \text{rad} > \frac{3\pi}{2} \)

Where \( \text{rad} \mid a > \text{rad} > b \) is the set of all radians such that \( a > \text{rad} > b \).
