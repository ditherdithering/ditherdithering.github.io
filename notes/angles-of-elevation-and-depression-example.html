If \( x = 50 \, \text{ft} \), \( \theta = 71.5^\circ \), what is \( y \)?

\[ \text{tan} \, 71.5 = \frac{y}{50} \]

\[ 50 (\text{tan} \, 71.5) = \frac{y}{50}(50) \]

\[ y \approx 149.43 \, \text{ft} \]

If \( y = 200 \, \text{yds} \), \( \theta = 60^\circ \), what is \( x \)?

\[ \text{cos} \, 60 = \frac{200}{x} \]

\[ (x) \text{cos} \, 60 = \frac{200}{x}(x) \]

\[ \frac{x (\text{cos} \, 60)}{\text{cos} \, 60} = \frac{200}{\text{cos} \, 60} \]

\[ x = 400 \, \text{yds} \]

If \( x = 67 \, \text{m} \), \( \theta = 70^\circ \), what is \( y \)?

\[ \text{tan} \, 70 = \frac{y}{67} \]

\[ 67 (\text{tan} \, 70) = \frac{y}{67}(67) \]

\[ y = 184 \, \text{m} \]
