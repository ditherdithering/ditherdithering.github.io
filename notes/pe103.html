Creating a Fitness Program

Involves designing a structured plan tailored to individual abilities, goals, and needs to improve overall health and fitness.

Begins with assessing current fitness levels, setting specific and achievable goals, and selecting exercises that target key components of fitness.

FITT

  • Frequency
    • How often
  • Intensity
    • How hard
  • Time
    • How long
  • Type
    • Variety

Principles of Fitness Program in Dance

PROVRBS
  • Progression
    • Gradual increase in intensity
  • Regularity
    • Consistency of exercise schedule
  • Overload
    • Pushing the body beyond the limit
  • Variety
    • Mixes exercises for motivation
  • Recovery
    • Ensure rest periods for repair and strength
  • Balance
    • Address all fitness areas equally
  • Specificity
    • Select exercises to match specific goals

A well-planned exercise program must also include safety awareness.

Introduction to Dance Genre

Elements

  • Body
    • Mobile figure or shape
  • Action
    • Movement of the body
  • Space
    • Area of the dance
  • Time
    • Rhythm or pattern (3/4, 2/4)
  • Energy
    • How the movement happens

Types of Bodily Shapes

  1. Symmetrical
  2. Asymmetrical

Group Shapes

  • Group body movement/formation

Subelements of Space

  1. Direction — movement
  2. Size — movement in varying action sizes
  3. Level — altitude of movement
  4. Focus — priority of vision

Qualities of Dance Energies

  1. Sustained — smooth flow
  2. Percussive — strong sharp action
  3. Vibratory — shaking
  4. Swinging — arced movement
  5. Suspended — hanging in the air
  6. Collapsing — giving into gravity

Types & Forms of Dance

  • Philippine Traditional Dance — folk and ethnic dances
  • Modern Dance — contemporary dance
  • Ballroom Dance — can include recreational or competitive
  • Cheerdance — group dance, acrobatic movement
  • Hip-Hop/Street Dance — energetic urban dance
  • Festival Dance — performed during cultural or local festivals
