Definition

Literature is defined as "a body of literary productions, either oral, written, or visual, containing imaginative language that realistically portrays thought, emotions, and experiences of the human condition."

Etymologically, it comes from the latin term "Litera", acquaintance of letters.

  • Literature may not be limited only to written works as oral forms such as chants, riddles, myths, and urban legends may also be classified as literature.
  • Literature uses imaginative or creative language that separates it from other literatures that utilize direct language (devoid of literary techniques or device) such as news report, or journal article.

Importance

Perspective

  • Literature presents human life and experience
  • In reading literature, one can be in other people's shoes, i.e feeling like what the other characters are feeling right now
    • This provides the reader a diverse range of beliefs, attitudes, and values of the world.

Understanding Society and the World

  • Literature can be a mirror to the culture where the reader belongs.
  • Writers use this to write their personal experience, context, and world view unique to their social world.

Opportunities for Expression

  • Writing literature gives an opportunity to give insights on matters or issues using the power of language.

Entertainment

  • Literature can provide entertainment.

Literary Standards

Intellectual Value

  • Literature can stimulate thinking skills, and enriches abstraction. Providing the fundamental truths of life and its nature.
  • i.e: Machiavelli's The Prince

Universality

  • Literature can appeal to everyone, regardless of biological and social aspects.
  • i.e: Chinua Achebe's Dead Men's Path

Artistry

  • Literature has an artistic appeal, therefore possessing a sense of beauty.
  • i.e: Dingdong Novenario's Ang Huling El Bimbo

Suggestiveness

  • Literature can provide nuances and hidden meanings, encouraging man to think further of its implied meanings, and symbolisms.
  • i.e: J. Neil's Garcia's The Conversion, Carlos A. Angeles' Landscape II

Spiritual Value

  • Literature elevates the soul and spirit to provide power to motivate and inspire from the morals and lessons.
  • i.e: Guy de Maupassant's The Necklace

Permanence

  • Literature is timeless, enduring through time.
  • i.e: The Little Prince

Style

  • Literature presents ways how man can see life by the formation of ideas, forms, and structures. Providing a memorable substance.
  • i.e: Adonis Durado's Balaki ko Day Samtang Nagsakay Ta'g Habal-habal
