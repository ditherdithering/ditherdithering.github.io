Human Reproductive System and Types

  • Produces Gametes
  • Nurtures offsprings during deployment
  • Produce Hormones
  • Store and Transport reproductive cells

    Male Reproductive System

  • Testis (Testicles)
    • Produces sperm and testosterone
  • Scrotum
    • Skin that encompasses the Testicles
  • Epididymis
    • Stores sperm
  • Vas deferens
    • Moves sperm to urethra during ejaculation
  • Seminal Vesicles
    • Produces fluids to protect and let the sperm swim.
  • Prostate Gland
    • Coats sperm with fluids called semen for nutrients and protection
  • Ejaculatory Ducts
    • Signals the brain that semen is ejaculating, producing pleasure
  • Bulbourethral Glands (Cowper's Gland)
    • Produces the pre-ejaculatory liquid
      • The liquid cleanses the urethra out of urine
  • Urethra
    • Passageway for both urine and semen to exit
  • Penis
    • Used to eliminate fluids in the male reproductive organs or a sperm delivery for sexual reproduction

      Formation of Sperm Cells / Spermatogenesis

      Starts in the Seminiferous tubules.
Stages Spermatogenesis (Process)
Multiplication Spermatogonia undergoes mitosis to multiply
Growth Spermatogonia enlarges to become Primary Spermatocytes
Maturation (Meiosis I) Primary Spermatocytes turn into Secondary Spermatocytes
Maturation (Meiosis II) Secondary Spermatocytes further mature into Spermatids
Spermiogenesis Spermatids form into Spermatozoa

Female Reproductive System

  • Ovary
    • Produces eggs (ova) and hormones like estrogen and progesterone
  • Fimbriae
  • Oviduct (Fallopian Tube)
    • Passageway for ovaries and for fertilization
  • Uterus
    • Place for fertilization and fetus growth
  • Cervix
    • Allows period blood to flow from uterus and pathway for sperm
  • Vagina
    • Exits for blood, baby during childbirth, and receives the penis during sexual reproduction
    • Naturally emits acidic fluids to protect the reproductive system
  • Vulva
    • The only external part of the Female Reproductive System

      Formation of Egg Cells / Oogenesis

      Starts in the Ovaries.
Stages Oogenesis (Process)
Multiplication Oogonia undergoes mitosis to multiply
Growth Oogonia grows to become Primary Oocytes
Maturation (Meiosis I) Primary Oocytes from two haploids cells, Secondary Oocyte and a polar body
Maturation (Meiosis II) Secondary Oocytes form into an ovum (Egg Cell) once it's fertilized
