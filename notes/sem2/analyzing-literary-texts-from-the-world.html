Elements

The examples are from The Bus Driver Who Wanted to be God, by Etgar Keret.

Characters

The people in the story.

The Bus Driver is punctual on its ideology, and with a desire to be a God.

Eddie is a sick man, lazy, and overly nice.

Happiness was a catalyst for Eddie and Bus Driver's principles to bend and change.

Setting

Location of the story.

Integral

Vividly described by the author.

Backdrop

Vague and general, multiple focal points of locations by the author.

Point of View

The perspective the story is told.

Internal

"I." (Narrator is the person in the story)

Omniscient

"All knowing." (Narrator is not in the story)

Limited Narrator

"Third Person." (Narrator is in the story)

Plot

The storyline of the story.

Dramatic

Chronological structure. Exposition -> Rising Action -> Climax -> Falling Action -> Resolution

Episodic

Each plot is different from different episode. Stands on its own

Parallel

Two narratives that are connected to each other.

Flashbacks

Recurring events from the past.

Conflict

Fighting against an idea, person, society, or the world.

Man vs Another

Fighting against a person, physically or ideologically.

Man vs Society

Fighting against a government or a group.

Man vs Nature

Fighting against the world or nature.

Man vs Self

Fighting against one's own principle or psyche.

Theme

The main idea of the story.

One's punctuality can be bended after another's deviates from its nature.

Tone and Mood

Usage of language to create an atmosphere or emotion.
