Introduction to Recreational Activities

Recreational Activities are actions performed specifically for fun, pleasure and amusement of others

Examples: Swimming, Bowling, Kite Flying, Birdwatching, Surfing, Dancing, Cycling, Water sports, Camping, Hiking, Scuba Diving

Benefits

Physical

  • improved physical fitness
  • weight management
  • better motor skills

Mental

  • stress reduction
  • improved concentration
  • boosts self esteem

Social

  • teamwork and cooperation
  • communication skills
  • self belonging

Emotional

  • increased emotional reliance
  • improved mood
  • sense of accomplishment

Risks in Aquatics

Aquatics refer to a range of recreational anf competitive activities in water.

Examples:

  • Diving

Potential Hazards/Dangers

  1. Drowning
    • Caused by inability to swim, fatigue, carelessness, or water conditions
  2. Injuries
    • From diving in shallow water, collisions, or improper equipment use
  3. Environmental Hazards
    • Currents, waves, and water temperature

Potential Risks

  1. Human Factors
    • Poor swimming skills
    • Alcohol consumption
  2. Environmental Factors
    • Poor visibility & weather changes
  3. Equipment Factors
    • Faulty or inappropriate equipments

Safety Measures

  • Always swim with lifeguards

Pool Etiquette and Entry

Etiquettes

  1. Shower before Entering
    • Removes sweat, dirt, & oils
  2. No running near the pool
  3. Respect other swimmers
  4. Avoid bringing glass items
  5. Follow lifeguard instructions
  6. No diving in shallow waters
  7. Don't monopolize pool equipment

Basic Swimming Skills

Bubbles

  • Purpose: Helps beginners be more comfortable submerged in water

Floating

  • Purpose: Teaches buoyancy and balancing in water

Treading

  • Purpose: Keeps swimmers upright and stationary in deep water

Gliding

  • Purpose: Helps positioning and streamlining for efficient movement

Flutter Kick

  • Purpose: Provides propulsion and balance in water
