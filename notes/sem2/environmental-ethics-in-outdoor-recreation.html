Leave no trace.

Leave no Trace Principle

  • Set of ethical guidelines designed to minimize human impact on the environment while enjoying the outdoors
  • Foundation for outdoor enjoyers, helping individuals protect and preserve natural environment for future generations
  • Ensures a sustainable relationship with nature

Principle 1

Plan for safety.

  • Research the area
  • Prepare for the environment
  • Respect local regulations & policies
  • Know the skills and equipment needed

Principle 2

Travel and camp on durable surfaces.

  • Durable surfaces include:
    • Established trails
    • Campsites
    • Rock
    • Gravel
    • Dry Grasses
  • Avoid creating new campsite, and camp in designated area.
  • Minimize impact to vegetation.

Principle 3

Dispose of Waste Properly.

  • Pack it in, pack it out.
  • Proper disposal of waste water and deposit solid human waste in cat holes, dug 6-8" deep at least 200' from water, camp, and trails.

Principle 4

Leave what you find.

  • Do not take natural objects.
  • Respect cultural and historical sites.
  • Avoid damaging trees and plants.

Principle 5

Minimize Campfire Impacts.

  • Consider alternatives such as camp stove.
  • Follow Local Fire Regulations.
    • Check fire restrictions or wildfire risk
  • Use Established Fire Rings or Fire Pans.
  • Keep fires small & use only local, dead wood

Principle 6

Respect wild life.

  • Observe from a distance.
  • Do not feed Wildlife.
  • Avoid wildlife during sensitive times, such as:
    • Mating
    • Nesting
    • Raising the Young

Principle 7

Be considerate with others.

  • Be courteous. Yield to others on the trail.
  • Respect others and protect the quality of their experience.
  • Let nature's sounds prevail. Avoid loud voices and noises.
