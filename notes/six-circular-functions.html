Definitions

Let \( \theta \) be the angle in standard position of the unit circle and \( P(x, y) \) be the point of the intersection of the terminal side of \( \theta \) and the unit circle.

  • Let the sine function as \( \sin \theta = y \)
  • Let \( \sin f(y) \to \sin \theta = y \)
  • Let \( \cos f(x) \to \cos \theta = x \)
  • Let \( f(\tan) \to \tan \theta = \frac{y}{x}, y \neq 0 \)
  • Let \( f(\cot) \to \cot \theta = \frac{x}{y}, x \neq 0 \)
  • Let \( f(\sec) \to \sec \theta = \frac{1}{x}, x \neq 0 \)
  • Let \( f(\csc) \to \csc \theta = \frac{1}{y}, y \neq 0 \)

Signs of the 6CF

Function Positive on Quadrant Negative on Quadrant
\( \sin \theta \) and \( \csc \theta \) I & II III & IV
\( \cos \theta \) and \( \sec \theta \) I & IV II & III
\( \tan \theta \) and \( \cot \theta \) I & III II & IV

Graphing sin & cos

  • Cycle: One complete repetition of the pattern.
  • Periodic Function: A wavelike function that repeats a pattern at regular intervals.

Amplitude: Vertical distance between the graph and the x-axis.

Period: X interval required to complete one cycle of a function.

General Equation (sin & cos)

\[ f(\theta) = a \sin b (\theta - h) + k \]

\[ g(\theta) = a \cos b (\theta - h) + k \]

Where \( a, b, h, k \neq 0 \) and are constants. If unknown, \( a, b = 1 \), \( h, k = 0 \).

Amplitude:

\( |a| \)

Range:

Interval of \( [k \pm |a|] \)

Period:

\[ p = \frac{2\pi}{|b|} \]

Phase Shifts of \( \sin \) and \( \cos \)

  • \( |h| \) shifts to the right if positive, left if negative.
  • \( |k| \) shifts upwards if positive, downwards if negative.

Tips

  • Consider the two general equations of \( \sin \) and \( \cos \).
