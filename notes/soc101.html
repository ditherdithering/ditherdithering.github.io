Culture

  • Way of living
  • Material/non-material
  • Capabilities acquired by man
  • Complex of traditional behavior nurtured by ancestors and consistently learned by generations

Culture by Edward Taylor

  • Complex whole, including knowledge, belief, art, law, morals, custom, and other capabilities and habits acquired by man as a member of society

Culture by Franz Boas

  • Totality of mental and physical reactions and activities, relationship to self, others, natural environment, and society

Manifestation of Cultural Diversity

Indigenous people

  • Group of people/homogeneous species identified by self-ascription who resisted political, social, and cultural inroads of colonization, nonindigenous religions and cultures and become differential from the majority of Filipinos (RA 8371)

Diversity in the Culture and the Arts

Art and Architecture:

  • Religious iconography and stone churches during Spanish colonial period
  • Artistic body adornments such as bead jewelry, body tattoos, and headdresses which were important indications of social status
  • Traditional art forms: bamboo and rattan weaving, wood carving, metal smithing, textile weaving

Literature:

  • Epic poems, legends, proverbs, songs, and riddles passed to generations through oral recitation and incantation in the various languages and dialects of the island
  • Jose Rizal's Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo
  • Florante at Laura

Music and Dance:

  • Tagalog folk songs, kumintang (warrior song), Kundiman (love song), Panambitan (courtship song), Talindaw (seafaring song), uyayi or hele (lullaby)
  • Antonio Molina, Felipe Padilla de Leon Sr., Eliseo Pajaro
  • Folk Dance Performers
  • Played on wind, string, and percussion instruments made from local materials such as kulibit, git-git

Paintings:

  • Juan Luna and Felix Hidalgo

Sculpture:

  • Ifugao sculptural wood carvings

Social Mobility

Change in position within the said social hierarchy

2 types:

  • Open system — status achieved through effort or merit
  • Closed system — status achieved through birth

Indicators:

  • Power — ability of one party to affect others' behavior
  • Prestige — reputation of self, subjectively evaluated by others
  • Wealth — education, income, occupation

Social Science

Anthropology

Greek for anthropos "human" and logos "knowledge". Study of humanity

4 fields:

  • Archaeology — examines past cultures via tangible remains
  • Cultural Anthropology — focuses on human culture with respect to social structure
  • Linguistics — anthropological study of language
  • Physical Anthropology — deals with understanding the causes of present human diversity

Political Science

Study of ideologies and the dynamics of governing and managing groups of people

Coined by Aristotle, Greek word polis, "city state"

Subfields:

  • Comparative politics — studies of nations
  • Constitutional law — study of legal systems
  • International relations — studies between nations
  • Political theory — studies classical and modern politics

Cultural Elements

  • Beliefs — ideas, viewpoints, and attitudes of others based on common sense, folklore, religion, or science
  • Language — shared set of verbal and written symbols for cultural transmission
  • Values — principles of behaviors and standards
  • Norms — unwritten rules that guide the behaviors of people
  • Symbols — verbal or nonverbal, used to tell meanings
  • Technology — applied science

Types of Societies

Foraging Societies

A.k.a Hunting and Gathering. Oldest and barebones form of society. Gender-based jobs (men hunt, women gather)

Pastoral and Horticultural

Cultural: animal domestication, cultivating small crops using small hand tools

Agricultural

Long-term settlements and political structures made, leading to inequality between powerful and powerless

Industrial

More urbanization in societies, rise of modern police forces, job opportunities in factories

Post Industrial

A.k.a Information Age. Priority to info, tech, and services

Theoretical Perspectives in Study of Culture and Society

  • Sociological Perspective — interpretation of things
  • Structural Functionalism — system with functioning parts and roles members have to achieve order and solidarity
  • Functionalism — defines culture as a whole that provides a system of meanings to what people do
  • Social Dysfunction — member of society not doing their roles
  • Social Construct — patterns of social relationships between people

Ethnocentrism and Cultural Relativism

  • Ethnocentrism — considering one's culture as superior to others
  • Cultural Relativism — considering all cultures as proportional to one another

Social Institutions

Structures in society

Family

Basic unit of society. Kinship — relations with members of society

Family kinships:

  • By blood — a.k.a consanguine kinships
  • By marriage — a.k.a affine kinship
  • By ritual — compadrazgo, godparenthood (ninongs & ninangs)

Types of families:

  • Nuclear — two married adults & children
  • Extended — mix of family members in a household
  • Single — consists of a parent and his/her children
  • Blended — two singles make one
  • Same-sex — homosexual couples

Perspectives (a.k.a structural functionalism):

  • Controls sexual reproduction
  • Provides social identities
  • Agents of socialization
  • Provides each other's needs
